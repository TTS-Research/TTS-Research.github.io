Parameter-Efficient Learning for Text-to-Speech Accent Adaptation

Abstract

This paper presents a parameter-efficient learning (PEL) to develop a low-resource accent adaptation for text-to-speech (TTS). A resource-efficient adaptation from a frozen pre-trained TTS model is developed by using only 1.2\% to 0.3\% of original trainable parameters to achieve competitive performance in voice synthesis. Motivated by a theoretical foundation of optimal transport, this study carries out PEL for TTS where feature distance between the pre-trained source domain and the low-resource target domain is maximized. Further, the unsupervised refinement via either sliced Wasserstein distance or maximum mean discrepancy is implemented to boost system performance. The merit of this work is demonstrated by fulfilling PEL solutions based on residual adapter learning, reprogramming, and bias-term fine-tuning when evaluating the Mandarin accent adaptation in unseen and seen conditions. Experiment results show proposed methods can achieve competitive naturalness with decoder fine-tuning, and the auxiliary unsupervised loss indeed improves the model performance.
